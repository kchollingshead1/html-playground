About

Pure metals. Liquefied. Molded. Hammered. Filed. I make each ring by hand at my shop in Oakland, CA.

When I receive your order, I begin by melting down pure sterling silver or titanium in our foundry. I then pour the metal into a mold that I make from a special type of clay (the molds are made and re-made for each customer). Then I begin the refining process: hammering and filing the ring into it's final shape. After sanding and brushing it down to a comfortable finish, I like to leave the ring with evidences that it's been worked on AND that it will be worked in.

When I was getting married I realized all I wanted was a light, comfortable ring that I could wear to work and work in the yard. But I couldn't find anything that fit my style. So I went to my shop, melted some pure sterling silver in my forge and I made my own. I had a lot of fun and I wanted to offer exactly what I wear: a hand poured, hammered ring that is made of solid sterling silver, filed down to size and sanded to a matte finish. No ring will look the same. It would be impossible.

If you have any questions or requests (think: thinner/thicker band (3mm, 4mm, 5mm or 6mm and any size, etc), just leave a comment in the conversation tab and I'll make it for you.
